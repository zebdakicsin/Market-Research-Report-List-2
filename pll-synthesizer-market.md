<p><p>A PLL (Phase-Locked Loop) synthesizer is an electronic circuit that generates stable and accurate frequency signals for various applications such as communication systems, electronic devices, and wireless modules. It consists of several components, including a voltage-controlled oscillator (VCO), a phase/frequency detector, a charge pump, and a loop filter. The VCO generates a signal with a variable frequency, which is compared to a reference signal by the phase/frequency detector. The charge pump and loop filter help adjust the VCO's frequency to match the reference signal, thus achieving synchronization.</p><p>The PLL synthesizer market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. <p><p>The PLL synthesizer market consists of two major types: Integer-N and Fractional-N synthesizers. Integer-N synthesizers generate output frequencies that are a multiple of the reference frequency, using integer values. They are relatively simpler and offer good performance but may exhibit spurious signals. Fractional-N synthesizers use fractional division techniques to generate output frequencies, allowing higher frequency resolution. They offer better phase noise performance but are more complex. Both types are widely used in various applications such as telecommunications, consumer electronics, and wireless communication systems.</p></p>
