<p><p>The Sedimentary Phosphate Rock market can be classified into three types: Marine Phosphate Deposits, Igneous Phosphate Deposits, and Others. Marine Phosphate Deposits consist of phosphate minerals formed in ancient marine environments, often found in coastal areas or under the sea. Igneous Phosphate Deposits are formed through volcanic activity and are found in igneous rocks. The "Others" category refers to phosphate rock deposits that do not fit into the marine or igneous classifications, such as weathered phosphatic sediments or residual deposits.</p></p>
