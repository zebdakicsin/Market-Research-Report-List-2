<p><p>Doughnuts are available in two main market styles: cake style and yeast style. Cake style doughnuts are denser and have a slightly crumbly texture, often made with baking powder or baking soda as leavening agents. They are often fried and have a sweeter taste. Yeast style doughnuts, on the other hand, are lighter and fluffier, made with yeast as a leavening agent. They are typically risen before being fried and have a more bread-like texture. Both styles offer a delicious treat with different textures and flavors to cater to various preferences.</p></p>
