<p><p>Soju, a popular Korean alcoholic beverage, can be classified into two main market types: distilled soju and diluted soju. Distilled soju undergoes a process where it is distilled to reach a higher alcohol content, usually above 25%. This type is known for its stronger taste and is commonly consumed straight or as a base for cocktails. On the other hand, diluted soju has a lower alcohol content, usually around 16-20%, achieved by mixing it with water during the production process. Diluted soju is smoother and milder in flavor, often enjoyed as a refreshing drink on its own or mixed with various flavors.</p></p>
