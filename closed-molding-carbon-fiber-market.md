<p><p>Closed molding carbon fiber refers to the process of manufacturing carbon fiber products without exposing them to the open air. There are two main types of closed molding carbon fiber markets: chopped carbon fiber and continuous carbon fiber. Chopped carbon fiber involves cutting carbon fiber strands or fabrics into short lengths and then mixing them with resin to create molded products. Continuous carbon fiber, on the other hand, uses long continuous carbon fiber strands that are impregnated with resin and then shaped into desired forms. These two markets cater to different manufacturing needs within the carbon fiber industry.</p></p>
