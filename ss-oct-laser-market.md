<p><p>SS-OCT (Swept-Source Optical Coherence Tomography) laser is a type of imaging technology that uses a tunable laser source to obtain high-resolution, real-time images of biological tissues. It is commonly used in ophthalmology for non-invasive visualization of various eye structures, such as the retina and anterior segment. Compared to traditional OCT systems, SS-OCT offers faster image acquisition speed, enhanced depth resolution, and reduced motion artifacts.</p><p>The SS-OCT laser market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 10.5% during the forecast period. The key driving factors behind this growth include the increasing incidence of ocular diseases, growing adoption of SS-OCT in ophthalmic clinics and hospitals, and advancements in imaging technology.</p><p>One of the major trends in the SS-OCT laser market is the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) algorithms into SS-OCT systems. AI-powered algorithms can analyze OCT images to detect and diagnose various eye conditions, including age-related macular degeneration, glaucoma, and diabetic retinopathy. This integration improves the accuracy and efficiency of diagnosis, leading to better patient outcomes.</p><p>Another trend is the miniaturization of SS-OCT devices. Portable and handheld SS-OCT systems are being developed for point-of-care diagnostics and remote monitoring of eye diseases. These compact systems offer convenience, ease-of-use, and cost-effectiveness, making them suitable for use in primary healthcare settings and underserved regions.</p><p>Furthermore, there is a growing demand for SS-OCT lasers in the research and development sector, as scientists and clinicians strive to improve understanding and treatment of eye diseases.
