<p><p>The solar cells and modules market is segmented into various types based on the technology used. Mono-Si modules are made from single-crystal silicon, providing high-efficiency and stability. Multi-Si modules are constructed from multi-crystalline silicon, offering lower cost but slightly lower efficiency. CdTe modules utilize thin films of cadmium telluride, making them cost-effective but less efficient. CIGS modules employ copper, indium, gallium, and selenium, combining flexibility and efficiency. A-Si modules use amorphous silicon, delivering excellent low-light performance. Lastly, the "Others" category includes innovative and emerging technologies in the market.</p></p>
