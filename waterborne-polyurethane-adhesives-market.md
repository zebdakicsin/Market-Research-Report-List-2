<p><strong>The Waterborne Polyurethane Adhesives Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Anionic Type</li><li>Cationic Type</li><li>Nonionic Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Waterborne polyurethane adhesives are classified into three types: anionic, cationic, and nonionic. </p><p>1. Anionic type: These adhesives possess negatively charged ions that disperse in water. They exhibit excellent adhesion to various substrates, including metal, plastic, and wood. Anionic adhesives are suitable for applications such as automotive, construction, and packaging.</p><p>2. Cationic type: These adhesives contain positively charged ions dispersed in water. They provide excellent bonding properties to difficult-to-bond substrates like glass, ceramics, and electronic components. Cationic adhesives are commonly used in the automotive, medical, and electronics industries.</p><p>3. Nonionic type: These adhesives do not carry any charge and are water-soluble due to their chemical structure. They offer good adhesion to various substrates and are especially suitable for applications requiring flexibility and durability. Nonionic adhesives find application in the footwear, textile, and furniture industries.</p></p>
