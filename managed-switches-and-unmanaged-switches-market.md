<p><p>Managed switches and unmanaged switches refer to the two types of network switches available in the market. </p><p>Managed switches are more advanced and offer greater control and configuration options. They can be monitored and managed remotely using network management software. With managed switches, administrators can control and prioritize network traffic, implement security measures, and ensure optimal performance. These switches are ideal for larger networks and businesses that require robust control and management features.</p><p>On the other hand, unmanaged switches are simpler and have limited configuration options. They are typically "plug-and-play" devices that do not require any additional setup. Unmanaged switches are suitable for small networks or home users who have basic networking needs and do not require advanced features or control over network traffic.</p><p>The managed switches and unmanaged switches market has been experiencing significant growth due to the increasing demand for scalable and efficient network infrastructure.
