<p><p>Amylose is a type of native cassava starch that contains a high percentage of linear amylose molecules. It is valued for its thickening and gelling properties, making it suitable for various food and industrial applications. Pullulan, on the other hand, is a polysaccharide derived from cassava starch fermentation. It is known for its exceptional film-forming and adhesive properties, commonly used in the food, pharmaceutical, and cosmetic industries. Both amylose and pullulan have diverse uses in different markets, offering unique functionalities and benefits.</p></p>
